The Great Star Coral forms large, dome-shaped colonies with a smooth surface. Its corallites are larger than those of the Boulder Star Coral, and the polyps are often brown, green, or reddish-brown.
Habitat: Commonly found in the Caribbean Sea, the Gulf of Mexico, and the western Atlantic, typically in deeper waters than Montastrea annularis.
Ecological Role: This coral is vital for reef structure and supports a diverse array of marine life.
