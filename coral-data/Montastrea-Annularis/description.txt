The Boulder Star Coral is a large, reef-building coral characterized by its massive, boulder-like formations. Its surface is covered with small, closely packed corallites, giving it a rough texture. The polyps are typically brown, tan, or green.
Habitat: Found in the Caribbean Sea and western Atlantic Ocean, usually at depths ranging from 1 to 50 meters.
Ecological Role: It is an essential species for reef construction and provides habitat for various marine organisms.
